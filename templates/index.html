Welcome to OntoVarsMiner

A web server for ontology-variants integrated analysis


OntoVarsMiner could help researchers identify somatic variants by uploading their own VCF files as database. Meanwhile, it is not limited to somatic variants, but can also be extended to include ontology like human phenotype ontology (HPO). Currently, OntoVarsMiner contains 130,000 records of ontology annotations, about 2,700,000 records of genes and more than 79 million variants data.


  • Upload: OntoVarsMiner accepts uncompressed or gzip-compatible compressed (*.gz) VCF files at this section
  • Download: Compressed (*.json) files which are derived from VCF files are available downloading at this section
  • Search: There are two types of search function in “search” section, one is disease search in which human disease is mandatory. After input a disease word, the result table with disease name, gene name, etc. When choosing a database (VCF files) and clicking the gene name at result table, the corresponding variations are shown in below. Another is custom search, flexible query is support for filtering variants.

GeneMiner could help researchers identify somatic variants by uploading their own VCF files as database. Meanwhile, it is not limited to somatic variants, but can also be extended to include ontology like human phenotype ontology (HPO). Currently, GeneMiner contains 130,000 records of ontology annotations, about 2,700,000 records of genes and more than 79 million variants data. By providing a user-friendly graphical user interface (GUI), researchers could directly deploy this in their personal computers or servers and perform the fast filtering by directly running a browser.
